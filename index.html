What is Schizophrenia? It's a serious mental disorder in where there is a distortion in the thoughts, emotions and behaviour of an individual which can lead to distortion of reality, inappropriate actions and faulty insight. (Also referred to as a Psychosis – loss of contact with reality). Schizophrenia can be acute (manifests as disrupted behaviour shown very suddenly) or chronic (characterised by gradual change over time). A person suffering from schizophrenia will lack insight into their condition – they will usually not be aware that they are ill. Schizophrenic type symptoms can affect up to about 1 % of the population. Onset of the disorder usually occurs between the late teens and mid-thirties.
